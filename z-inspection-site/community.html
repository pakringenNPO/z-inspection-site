The Z-Inspection® Network

Z-Inspection® brings together a diverse, international community of researchers, practitioners, policymakers, and domain experts. Members contribute to assessments, research, education, and methodology development across multiple sectors and regions.

The community includes specialists in artificial intelligence, machine learning, data science, bioethics, law, public policy, clinical medicine, social sciences, agriculture, education, and environmental science. This diversity is essential to the methodology, which requires multiple perspectives to identify ethical tensions in AI systems.

Conferences

The Z-Inspection® initiative organizes international conferences to advance the field of trustworthy AI assessment and bring together practitioners, researchers, and policymakers.

Venice 2023

First international Z-Inspection® conference. Focus on establishing the community and sharing early case study results across healthcare and government applications.

Hamburg 2024

Expanded program with workshops on the EU AI Act, methodology training, and presentations from completed assessments in education and agriculture.

Seoul 2025

Global conference with participants from Asia, Europe, North America, and Africa. Focus on scaling trustworthy AI assessment across regulatory frameworks worldwide.

EU-Funded Projects

Z-Inspection® is actively used and developed within several EU Horizon Europe projects, demonstrating its applicability and impact in large-scale research and innovation contexts.

MANOLO

Edge cloud AI orchestration. Z-Inspection® is used to assess trustworthiness of AI systems deployed at the edge, including EEG sleep monitoring and mobile computer vision platforms.

ACHILLES

Applied AI ethics. Developing and testing ethical assessment frameworks for AI systems in practice, with Z-Inspection® as a core methodology.

SALL

LLM-based self-assessment for Trustworthy AI. Building an automated tool that leverages large language models to support Z-Inspection® assessments at scale.

KOREA

AI for polygenic risk scores in Type 2 Diabetes. Medical assessment led by Pedro Kringen and Megan Coffee, applying Z-Inspection® to genomics-based clinical decision support.

FEHLS

Federated AI for clinical decision support. Assessment of federated learning systems used in hospitals across Norway and Denmark.

Education and Training

The Z-Inspection® initiative offers training and education programs to build capacity in trustworthy AI assessment worldwide.

  • xAIM Course at the University of Pavia. A structured course on the Z-Inspection® methodology, co-taught by initiative members. Available to graduate students and professionals.
  • Certified Instructors program. Trained assessors who can lead Z-Inspection® assessments independently for their organizations or as consultants.
  • Workshops and seminars at international conferences and institutions, including the University of Göttingen (Max Planck) and Arcada University of Applied Sciences.

Get Involved

The Z-Inspection® community welcomes new members who can contribute expertise in AI, ethics, law, medicine, or domain-specific knowledge. If you are interested in joining the network, participating in assessments, or hosting a training event, please contact us.
