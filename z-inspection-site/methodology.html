Methodology

How Z-Inspection® works in practice

What is Z-Inspection®?

Z-Inspection® is a structured process for answering a simple but important question: Can we trust this AI system?

The process brings together a team of people with different expertise, including AI engineers, ethicists, lawyers, clinicians, and domain specialists. Together, they examine how an AI system works in real-world situations and identify where problems might arise. Problems could be technical (the system fails under certain conditions), ethical (the system treats groups unfairly), legal (the system violates data protection rules), or social (the system undermines human autonomy).

The methodology was first published in IEEE Transactions on Technology and Society in 2021 and has since been applied to AI systems in healthcare, government, education, agriculture, and genomics. It is listed in the OECD Catalogue of AI Tools and Metrics.

The Three Phases

Phase 1. Set Up

Define what AI system is being assessed and in which context. Assemble a team with the right mix of expertise. Make sure there is access to documentation, developers, and end users. Agree on scope, timeline, and deliverables.

Phase 2. Assess

Describe realistic situations where the AI system is used (socio-technical scenarios). For each scenario, check the system against seven trustworthiness requirements from the EU guidelines. Identify ethical tensions, which are situations where principles conflict or where someone's interests are at risk.

Phase 3. Resolve

Prioritize the identified tensions by severity. Propose concrete recommendations, both technical and organizational. Deliver a structured report. Establish how the system should be monitored going forward, because trustworthiness is not a one-time check.

The Seven Requirements

Every Z-Inspection® assessment evaluates the AI system against seven requirements defined by the EU High-Level Expert Group on AI. These are checked systematically using ALTAI (Assessment List for Trustworthy AI).

1

Human Agency and Oversight

People must remain in control. The system should support human decision-making, not replace it without consent.

2

Technical Robustness and Safety

The system must work reliably, handle errors gracefully, and resist attacks or manipulation.

3

Privacy and Data Governance

Personal data must be handled correctly. The system must comply with regulations like the GDPR.

4

Transparency

Users should understand what the system does, how it reaches its conclusions, and what its limitations are.

5

Diversity, Non-discrimination, and Fairness

The system must not discriminate. It should be accessible and designed with diverse users in mind.

6

Societal and Environmental Well-being

The system should benefit society and minimize environmental impact.

7

Accountability

Someone must be responsible. The system should be auditable, and affected parties should have access to redress.

Regulatory Alignment

Z-Inspection® aligns with major regulatory frameworks for AI governance.

  • EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689). The Act requires conformity assessments for high-risk AI systems. Z-Inspection® provides a proven framework for conducting these assessments. Full text on EUR-Lex
  • EU Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI. The seven requirements above come directly from these guidelines, published by the High-Level Expert Group on AI in 2019.
  • ALTAI. The Assessment List for Trustworthy AI is a practical self-assessment checklist from the European Commission. Z-Inspection® uses ALTAI as a structured tool within the assessment process.
